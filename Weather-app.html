Weather app study

Role: UI Designer, prototyping

Scope: My personal mobile version of a Weather app, a Figma exercise

Context

In April 2024, it was like 3 months I had been initiated to Figma, so I decided to explore the vast UI design world through the good old art of "copying". I found out an interesting fully-guided tutorial on YouTube, and I gave it a try.

At that time, Glassmorphism was considered an "experimental" or niche aesthetic for many designers. This study allowed me to explore its potential loooong before it became the dominant 2025-2026 trend ("Effects > Glass" in Figma didn't even exist!). Ahhh the magical "Liquid glass": now massively omnipresent thanks to iOS and worshipped by everyone, yet microscopically - or not at all - compliant with A11Y standards!

The UI kit

What I learned:

  • Design system foundations - I learned the importance of a clean and organized Figma file;
  • Component architecture - I focused on atomic design principles to create reusable components;
  • Mobile prototyping & interactions - I trained on user flow transitions to simulate a realistic app experience;
  • Using visual effects -I experimented with complex background blurs, multi-layered shadows, and specific opacity settings to achieve a balanced, high-fidelity and modern aesthetic;
  • Card design - I focused on data visualisation within different cards, learning how to present weather metrics through a clear visual hierarchy that is both aesthetically pleasing and easy to scan;
  • UI/UX balance - Beyond the "copying" exercise, I analyzed the layout's usability, ensuring that the Glassmorphism effects didn't interfere with the contrast and accessibility of the dynamic weather data.
